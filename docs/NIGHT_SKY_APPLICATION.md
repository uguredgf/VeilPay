## Project description

> VeilPay is a privacy-preserving payroll workflow for companies and distributed teams. Employers prepare payroll batches, employees claim payments through one-time credentials and a Midnight wallet, and auditors review compliance evidence without exposing individual salary data. The current MVP includes working employer, employee and compliance portals, a persistent backend, CSV validation and Lace wallet connectivity. The Lace integration discovers installed Midnight wallets, requests permission, validates a compatible network and reads the user's shielded address. Midnight transaction signing, proof execution and submission remain explicitly labelled simulation milestones while we complete Compact compilation, generated bindings and preprod deployment.

## Problem and differentiation

> Public blockchain payment rails can expose salary amounts, payment timing and employer-employee relationships. Traditional payroll systems protect data but do not provide independently verifiable on-chain settlement, while transparent on-chain payroll creates confidentiality and compliance risk. VeilPay is designed around selective disclosure from the beginning: private payroll commitments, one-time employee claims and audit workflows that reveal only the evidence a reviewer needs. Our focus is enterprise payroll operations and compliance usability, not a general-purpose privacy transfer tool.

## How Midnight privacy technology enhances the business

> Payroll requires confidentiality, provability and predictable operating costs. Midnight's hybrid ledger can preserve durable settlement state while protecting salary inputs and employee relationships. Compact circuits and zero-knowledge proofs can prove that a claim belongs to a valid payroll batch without revealing its amount; nullifiers can prevent double claims; and selective disclosure can reveal only policy evidence to an authorized auditor. The NIGHT/DUST resource model also separates capital from transaction resources, supporting more predictable enterprise operations and the possibility of employer-funded wallet interactions. These capabilities make privacy part of the payroll settlement architecture rather than an off-chain promise.
