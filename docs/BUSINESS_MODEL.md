# VeilPay Business Model

## Revenue model

VeilPay is designed around a hybrid B2B model rather than a project token.

1. Platform subscription: monthly or annual employer plans for payroll administration, compliance workflows, team access and reporting.
2. Usage revenue: variable fees based on completed payroll batches, active employees and proof workload.
3. Enterprise services: paid integrations, compliance configuration, support and private deployment options for payroll providers and larger organizations.

## Go-to-market sequence

1. Start with crypto-native teams, DAOs and foundations that already pay distributed contributors and experience public-ledger privacy problems.
2. Validate workflow, pricing and compliance requirements through design-partner interviews and a small pilot.
3. Expand through payroll providers and treasury platforms that can embed VeilPay into existing employer workflows.

## Pricing discipline

No price point is presented as validated yet. Subscription tiers, usage units, gross margin assumptions and enterprise service pricing should be tested with real buyers before inclusion in investor-facing forecasts.

## Financial application materials

The Night Sky criteria require a capitalization table, cash-flow forecast and profit-and-loss statement. Those files must be prepared from real ownership data and applicant-approved assumptions. The model should separate recurring subscription revenue, variable usage revenue, enterprise service revenue, infrastructure costs, payroll, legal/compliance and customer acquisition expenses.
